Career

Kofi Annan had a distinguished career in international diplomacy. He began working for the United Nations in 1962, and over the years held a variety of leadership positions. Annan served as the UN Secretary-General from 1997 to 2006, and was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2001 for his work promoting peace and human rights. He also served as the Chair of the Kofi Annan Foundation, which works to promote sustainable development, peace, and human rights.
